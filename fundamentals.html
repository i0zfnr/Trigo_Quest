Fundamental Trigonometric Functions

1.1.1 Trigonometric Functions Formulas

TrigonometricFunction
  • Trigonometric functions (sin, cos, tan, cosec, sec, cot) are defined using the sides of a right-angled triangle:
  • opposite
  • adjacent
  • hypotenuse
    • Sine is written as sin, cosine is written as cos, tangent is denoted by tan, secant is denoted by sec, cosecant is abbreviated as cosec, and cotangent is abbreviated as cot.

    Reciprocal pairs: sin ↔ cosec, cos ↔ sec, tan ↔ cot

    1.1.2 Principal Values (Unit Circle)

    • Trigonometric functions have a domainθ (degree/radians)
    • Principal values are also referred to as standard values of trig functions.
    • Frequently used in calculations and identities.
    • These values are derived from the unit circle and satisfy all trigonometric formulas.

    1.1.3 Trigonometric Graphs

    • Graphs of trigonometric functions have thedomain value of θ represented on the horizontal x-axisand range value represented the vertical y-axis.
    • sinθ & tanθ graphs pass through the origin
    • Range of sinθ & cosθ is limited to [-1, 1]

    1.1.4 Trig Functions in Four Quadrants

    • The angle θ is an acute angle and is measured with reference to the positive x-axis, in the anticlockwise direction.
    • these trig functions have different numeric signs (+ or -) in the different quadrants, which are based on the positive or negative axis of the quadrant.
    • functions of sinθ, cosecθ are positive in quadrants I and II and are negative in quadrants III and IV.
    • All the trigonometric functions have a positive range in the first quadrant.

    1.1.5 Reference Angles

    The reference angle is the smallest, positive, acute angle formed by the terminal side of θ and the horizontal axis. It simplifies calculations for angles in any quadrant.
